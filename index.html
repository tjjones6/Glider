Math 415 Applied Dynamical Systems: Glider

Consider a glider flying at speed \(v(t)\) at an angle \(\theta(t) \) to the horizontal. Its motion is governed by: $$ \begin{cases} m\dot{v}=-mg\sin(\theta)-\frac{1}{2}\rho C_D Sv^2,\\ mv\dot{\theta}=-mg\cos(\theta)+\frac{1}{2}\rho C_L Sv^2, \end{cases} $$ for the glider mass m, wing surface area S, gravitational acceleration g, air density œÅ, drag coefficient \(C_D\), and lift coefficient \(C_L\).

First, let's non-dimensionalize the governing equations to analyze the fundamental dynamics of the system without being overly influenced by specific values for mass, surface area, gravitational acceleration, and air density. $$ \begin{cases} \frac{v}{x^*}=x \Rightarrow dv=x^*dx \\ \frac{t}{\tau}=t^* \Rightarrow dt=t^*d\tau \end{cases} $$ Note that v is the dimensional speed, x is the dimensionless speed, \(x^*\) is the characteristic speed. Similarly, t is dimensional time, \(\tau\) is the dimensionless time, and \(t^*\) is the dimensionless time. Now let's rewrite our original (dimensional) equations of motion. $$ m\frac{x^*}{t^*}\frac{dx}{d\tau} = -mg\sin(\theta)-\frac{1}{2}\rho C_D S(xx^*)^2 $$ $$ \Rightarrow \frac{dx}{d\tau} = -\frac{t^*}{x^*}g\sin(\theta)-\frac{t^*}{2m}\rho C_D S x^2 x^* \tag{I} $$ $$ mxx^*t^*\frac{d\theta}{d\tau} = -mg\cos(\theta)+\frac{1}{2}\rho C_L S(xx^*)^2 $$ $$ \Rightarrow x\frac{d\theta}{d\tau} = -\frac{g}{x^*t^*}\cos(\theta)+\frac{\rho C_L S}{2mt^*}x^2x^* \tag{II} $$

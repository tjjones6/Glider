Math 415 Applied Dynamical Systems: Glider

Tyler Jones

Consider a glider flying at speed \(v(t)\) at an angle \(\theta(t) \) to the horizontal. Its motion is governed by: $$ m\dot{v}=-mg\sin(\theta)-\frac{1}{2}\rho C_D Sv^2,\tag{I}$$ $$ mv\dot{\theta}=-mg\cos(\theta)+\frac{1}{2}\rho C_L Sv^2,\tag{II} $$ for the glider mass m, wing surface area S, gravitational acceleration g, air density œÅ, drag coefficient \(C_D\), and lift coefficient \(C_L\).

First, let's non-dimensionalize the governing equations to analyze the fundamental dynamics of the system without being overly influenced by specific values for mass, surface area, gravitational acceleration, and air density. $$ \begin{cases} \frac{v}{x^*}=x \Rightarrow dv=x^*dx \\ \frac{t}{\tau}=t^* \Rightarrow dt=t^*d\tau \end{cases} $$ Note that v is the dimensional speed, x is the dimensionless speed, \(x^*\) is the characteristic speed. Similarly, t is dimensional time, \(\tau\) is the dimensionless time, and \(t^*\) is the dimensionless time. Now let's rewrite our original (dimensional) equations of motion. $$ m\frac{x^*}{t^*}\frac{dx}{d\tau} = -mg\sin(\theta)-\frac{1}{2}\rho C_D S(xx^*)^2 $$ $$ \Rightarrow \frac{dx}{d\tau} = -\frac{t^*}{x^*}g\sin(\theta)-\frac{t^*}{2m}\rho C_D S x^2 x^* \tag{III} $$ $$ mxx^*t^*\frac{d\theta}{d\tau} = -mg\cos(\theta)+\frac{1}{2}\rho C_L S(xx^*)^2 $$ $$ \Rightarrow x\frac{d\theta}{d\tau} = -\frac{g}{x^*t^*}\cos(\theta)+\frac{\rho C_L S}{2mt^*}x^2x^* \tag{IV} $$ Now that we have non-dimensionalized the original equations of motion (I-II), we will verify that our new set of equations (III-IV) are consistent with the original equations. This will be accomplished by comparing each term as follows for equations (I-III): $$ D\equiv \frac{C_D}{C_L} $$ $$ -\frac{t^*}{x^*}g\sin(\theta) - \frac{t^*}{2m}\rho C_DSx^2x^*=-\sin(\theta)-\frac{C_D}{C_L}x^2 $$ $$ \Rightarrow \begin{cases} -\frac{t^*}{x^*}g\sin(\theta)=-\sin(\theta)\\ - \frac{t^*}{2m}\rho C_DSx^2x^*=-\frac{C_D}{C_L}x^2 \end{cases} \Rightarrow \begin{cases} x^*=\sqrt{\frac{2mg}{\rho SC_L}}\\ t^* = \frac{x^*}{g} \end{cases} $$ $$ \boxed{ \begin{cases} \frac{dx}{d\tau}=-\sin(\theta)-Dx^2\\ x\frac{d\theta}{d\tau}=-\cos(\theta)+x^2 \end{cases} } $$

Now that we have our system in a dimenionless form for dimensionless \(\tau\), dimensionless speed \( x(\tau)\), and drag ratio D, let's analyze the system with the case of negligible drag (\(D=0\)). Since we are neglecting drag, let's show that \( E(x,\theta)=x^3-3x\cos(\theta)\) is a conserved quantity. To do so, we will ensure that the system is not dissipative by calculating the time rate of change of the energy. $$ \frac{dE}{d\tau}=3x^2\frac{dx}{d\tau}-3\Big(\frac{dx}{d\tau}\cos\theta-x\sin\theta\frac{d\theta}{d\tau}\Big) $$ $$ =3\frac{dx}{d\tau}(x^2-\cos\theta)+3x\sin\theta\frac{d\theta}{d\tau} $$ $$ =-3\sin\theta(x^2-\cos\theta)+3\sin\theta(x^2-\cos\theta)=0 $$ $$\boxed{\frac{dE}{d\tau}=0}$$ Since we have shown that our system is not dissipative (conserved), we can continue with our bifurcation analysis. First, we will need to find and classify all fixed points. $$ \begin{cases} \dot{x}=-\sin\theta\\ \dot{\theta}=-\frac{\cos\theta}{x}+x \end{cases} \Rightarrow \begin{cases} \sin\theta=0\\ \cos\theta=x^2 \end{cases} \Rightarrow \begin{cases} \theta^*=2\pi n,n\in \mathbb{R}\\ x^*=\pm 1 \end{cases} $$ $$ \boxed{(x_c,\theta_c)=(\pm 1,2\pi n)} $$
